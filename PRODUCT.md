# Product

## Register

product

## Users

The primary users are administrators, student affairs staff, and students at Minya National University.

Administrators and student affairs staff use the system during operational academic workflows: managing student records, entering and reviewing grades, organizing departments and subjects, searching records, and generating reports efficiently without cognitive overload. They need clarity, speed, and confidence because data quality affects official academic records.

Students use the system to track academic progress through their dashboard, profile, grades, and transcripts. They need a clear, motivating, modern experience that makes academic status understandable without making the interface feel juvenile.

## Product Purpose

This product is a Student Management System for Minya National University. It centralizes student records, grade management, academic reporting, and student-facing progress views in one authenticated product.

Success means administrators can complete record and grade workflows quickly and accurately, student affairs can generate useful reports without clutter, and students can understand their academic progress at a glance through a polished, trustworthy dashboard.

## Brand Personality

Premium, dynamic, efficient.

The product should feel modern and high-confidence while remaining operationally focused. It should communicate institutional authority without becoming stiff, and it should give students a sense of academic momentum without drifting into consumer-app playfulness.

## Anti-references

It must not feel like a dense legacy ERP, a generic AI-generated Bootstrap template, or a boring or childish academic portal.

It must not feel cheap or cluttered. Avoid visual noise, low-quality stock-dashboard patterns, decorative effects without functional purpose, and any layout that makes academic data harder to scan.

## Design Principles

1. **Official confidence first.** Academic records, grades, and transcripts must feel trustworthy, legible, and institutionally credible.
2. **Reduce cognitive load.** Dense administrative work should be organized around scanning, comparison, and decisive action, not decorative panels.
3. **Progress should feel visible.** Student views should make academic standing clear and motivating without turning the product into a childish portal.
4. **Premium through restraint and precision.** The interface should earn its premium feel through spacing, hierarchy, contrast, and motion discipline, not clutter or ornament.
5. **Bilingual by construction.** Arabic and English support must be integral to layout, navigation, alignment, spacing, and text direction.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AA compliance is required.

Seamless Arabic and English support is strictly mandatory, including reliable RTL/LTR switching across navigation, tables, forms, dashboards, reports, and transcripts.

The product should support a modern high-contrast dark mode aesthetic without sacrificing readability. Critical data such as grades, attendance, status, risk, and success indicators must use color-blind-safe status treatment, pairing color with text, icons, shape, or position so meaning is never color-only.
